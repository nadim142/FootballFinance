My Personal Story

I have been a fan of football my entire life, playing since as early as I can remember. Despite growing up in America with the intense popularity and fandom surrounding the NFL, NBA, NHL, MLB, and college athletics, I still found myself being the only friend ever wanting to watch what I then called soccer. As I got older and realized I had to support a team of my own in Europe, when I was around 8 or 9 years old (2012-ish), I started watching the Premier League, which further enhanced this need for a team. At the time, my childhood bedroom was this sky blue color that I had picked out when our house was built in 2007. I then found myself on my family's computer scrolling through all of the Premier League teams and found a team that somehow had the exact same color and I instantly became attached. That team being Manchester City.

Now, I know that I am probably the luckiest fan on Earth for choosing that team at such a young age and now know about them being bought by the Abu Dhabi United Group. However, at that time, I had no idea about the business side of football, or even that players were paid. Some might call me a bandwagon, or that I picked the best team in the Premier League at the time, but 8-year-old me will choose to play the innocent card here and state that I didn’t have a clue in the world what any of that meant.

Additionally, the amount of fans in America that I know now are Chelsea, Liverpool, Man United, Barcelona, and Real Madrid fans for reasons only of success. I would like to say I am less of a bandwagon than them and have always stayed a Manchester City fan. Following my new team, an iconic moment happened to go on at the end of the season which only propelled me to be an even bigger fan. That moment being… AGUEROOOOOOOO. Since that moment, I became a huge supporter of Manchester City and the game of football itself, fulfilling my childhood dream of going to a Man City home match at the Etihad for the first time in 2024.

Being from Detroit, Michigan, I grew up and still am to this day a fan of the Detroit Lions (NFL), Detroit Tigers (MLB), Detroit Pistons (NBA), and Detroit Red Wings (NHL). As some of you may be aware, they all sucked growing up, but I have always loved that deep fan community culture that a lot of Detroit fans share and have related on during this dark age.

My parents both grew up football players and fans, with my mom playing for her university on a scholarship after being one of the youngest captains for her high school team ever beforehand as well as being a varsity player as a freshman (15-year-old playing on a 17- and 18-year-old team).

Now that you read my story, and I greatly appreciate you doing so… I encourage you to read and listen to the stories of fans all across the globe, from Detroit City FC to Santos FC to Manchester City FC and many more.

Why Fans Are the Heart of Football

All the financials and money behind football wouldn't be possible without **us, the fans**. From ticket sales, merchandise purchases, sponsorships, to the global reach of football clubs, **fans fuel the football business**. So keep watching, keep supporting your local club, and keep spreading the love for the game!
